Order of Operations

Order of Operations is the sequence in which mathematical operations should be performed to get the correct result.

PEMDAS Rule

The standard order of operations is remembered using the acronym PEMDAS:

  • P
  • E (powers and roots)
  • MD (Multiplication and Division from left to right)
  • AS (Addition and Subtraction from left to right)

Examples

Example: Solve 8 + 2 × (3 + 1)

  1. Parentheses: (3 + 1) = 4
  2. Multiply: 2 × 4 = 8
  3. Add: 8 + 8 = 16

Example: Solve (6 + 2) ÷ 2²

  1. Parentheses: 6 + 2 = 8
  2. Exponents: 2² = 4
  3. Divide: 8 ÷ 4 = 2

Practice Problems

1. Solve: 5 + 3 × 2

3 × 2 = 6 → 5 + 6 = 11

2. Solve: (4 + 6) × 3

4 + 6 = 10 → 10 × 3 = 30

3. Solve: 18 ÷ (3 × 2)

3 × 2 = 6 → 18 ÷ 6 = 3
