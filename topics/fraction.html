Understanding Fractions

Fractions represent parts of a whole or a ratio between two numbers.

Basic Concepts of Fractions

Definition

A fraction is expressed as \frac{a}{b}, where a is the numerator and b \neq 0 is the denominator.

Types of Fractions

  • Proper fraction: numerator is less than denominator, e.g., \frac{3}{4}.
  • Improper fraction: numerator is greater than or equal to denominator, e.g., \frac{5}{3}.
  • Mixed number: a whole number plus a proper fraction, e.g., 1 \frac{2}{5}.

Equivalent Fractions

Fractions that represent the same value, e.g., \frac{1}{2} = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{50}{100}.

Example 1: Simplify \frac{8}{12}.

Solution: Divide numerator and denominator by their GCD 4: \frac{8 \div 4}{12 \div 4} = \frac{2}{3}.

Example 2: Convert the mixed number 2 \frac{3}{5} into an improper fraction.

Solution: Multiply whole number by denominator and add numerator: \frac{2 \times 5 + 3}{5} = \frac{13}{5}.

Step-by-Step Methods

Simplifying Fractions

  1. Find the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) of numerator and denominator.
  2. Divide numerator and denominator by the GCD.
  3. The result is the simplified fraction.

Adding Fractions

  1. Find the Least Common Denominator (LCD).
  2. Convert fractions to equivalent fractions with the LCD.
  3. Add the numerators and keep the denominator same.
  4. Simplify the resulting fraction if needed.

Practice Problems

1. Simplify \frac{15}{25}.

Solution:

GCD of 15 and 25 is 5, so \frac{15 \div 5}{25 \div 5} = \frac{3}{5}.

2. Add \frac{1}{4} + \frac{1}{6}.

Solution:

LCD of 4 and 6 is 12. Convert: \frac{3}{12} + \frac{2}{12} = \frac{5}{12}.

3. Convert 3 \frac{1}{2} to an improper fraction.

Solution:

\frac{3 \times 2 + 1}{2} = \frac{7}{2}.

4. Multiply \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{4}{5}.

Solution:

Multiply numerators and denominators: \frac{2 \times 4}{3 \times 5} = \frac{8}{15}.
