Ancient Mathematicians

Euclid

c. 300 BCE
Known as the "Father of Geometry." Wrote "Elements," the most influential mathematical work of all time. Established the foundations of geometry and number theory.

Archimedes

c. 287-212 BCE
Made significant contributions to geometry, calculus, and physics. Discovered principles of buoyancy and developed methods for calculating areas and volumes.

Pythagoras

c. 570-495 BCE
Famous for the Pythagorean theorem. Founded a school of mathematics and philosophy. Made important contributions to number theory and geometry.

Thales of Miletus

c. 624-546 BCE
One of the first known mathematicians. Proved geometric theorems and introduced deductive reasoning to mathematics.

Diophantus

c. 200-284 CE
Known as the "Father of Algebra." Wrote "Arithmetica," which introduced algebraic notation and methods.

Medieval Mathematicians

Al-Samawal (c. 1130–1180)

Islamic mathematician who extended the work of Al-Khwarizmi and used algebraic notation.

Bhaskara II (1114–1185)

Indian mathematician and astronomer known for his book *Siddhanta Shiromani*, including early concepts of calculus.

Fibonacci (1170–1250)

Italian mathematician known for the Fibonacci sequence and introducing Hindu-Arabic numerals to Europe.

Omar Khayyam (1048–1131)

Persian polymath known for classifying cubic equations and calendar reform.

Al-Khwarizmi

c. 780-850 CE
Known as the "Father of Algebra." Introduced the decimal system to the Western world. Wrote the first book on algebra.

Fibonacci

c. 1170-1250
Introduced the Fibonacci sequence to Western mathematics. Popularized the Hindu-Arabic numeral system in Europe.

Omar Khayyam

1048-1131
Made significant contributions to algebra and geometry. Solved cubic equations and wrote influential works on mathematics.

Brahmagupta

598-668 CE
Introduced the concept of zero and negative numbers. Made fundamental contributions to algebra and astronomy.

Renaissance and Early Modern Mathematicians

Leonardo da Vinci

1452-1519
Applied mathematics to art and engineering. Made significant contributions to geometry and perspective.

René Descartes

1596-1650
Developed Cartesian geometry. Created the coordinate system. Made fundamental contributions to algebra and geometry.

Pierre de Fermat

1607-1665
Founded number theory. Known for Fermat's Last Theorem. Made significant contributions to probability theory.

Blaise Pascal

1623-1662
Made fundamental contributions to probability theory. Invented the mechanical calculator. Developed Pascal's triangle.

Isaac Newton

1643-1727
Co-inventor of calculus. Made fundamental contributions to physics and mathematics. Developed the binomial theorem.

Gottfried Leibniz

1646-1716
Co-inventor of calculus. Developed the binary number system. Made significant contributions to logic and mathematics.

18th Century Mathematicians

Leonhard Euler

1707-1783
One of the greatest mathematicians of all time. Made fundamental contributions to calculus, graph theory, and number theory.

Joseph-Louis Lagrange

1736-1813
Made significant contributions to calculus, number theory, and mechanics. Developed Lagrangian mechanics.

Pierre-Simon Laplace

1749-1827
Made fundamental contributions to probability theory and celestial mechanics. Developed Laplace transforms.

Adrien-Marie Legendre

1752-1833
Made significant contributions to number theory and elliptic functions. Developed Legendre polynomials.

19th Century Mathematicians

Carl Friedrich Gauss

1777-1855
Known as the "Prince of Mathematicians." Made fundamental contributions to number theory, algebra, and statistics.

Évariste Galois

1811-1832
Founded group theory. Made fundamental contributions to abstract algebra. Solved the problem of solvability of polynomial equations.

Bernhard Riemann

1826-1866
Developed Riemannian geometry. Made fundamental contributions to analysis and number theory. Formulated the Riemann hypothesis.

Augustin-Louis Cauchy

1789-1857
Made fundamental contributions to analysis and complex analysis. Developed the Cauchy integral theorem.

Niels Henrik Abel

1802-1829
Made fundamental contributions to algebra and analysis. Proved the impossibility of solving quintic equations by radicals.

Sophie Germain

1776-1831
Made significant contributions to number theory and elasticity theory. Worked on Fermat's Last Theorem.

20th Century Mathematicians

David Hilbert

1862-1943
One of the most influential mathematicians. Formulated 23 problems that shaped 20th-century mathematics.

Henri Poincaré

1854-1912
Made fundamental contributions to topology, celestial mechanics, and relativity theory.

John von Neumann

1903-1957
Made fundamental contributions to game theory, quantum mechanics, and computer science.

Alan Turing

1912-1954
Father of theoretical computer science. Made fundamental contributions to cryptography and artificial intelligence.

Kurt Gödel

1906-1978
Proved the incompleteness theorems. Made fundamental contributions to mathematical logic.

Emmy Noether

1882-1935
Made fundamental contributions to abstract algebra and theoretical physics. Developed Noether's theorem.

Alexander Grothendieck

1928-2014
Revolutionized algebraic geometry. Made fundamental contributions to category theory and topology.

Paul Erdős

1913-1996
One of the most prolific mathematicians. Made fundamental contributions to number theory and combinatorics.

Contemporary Mathematicians

Andrew Wiles

1953-present
Proved Fermat's Last Theorem. Made fundamental contributions to number theory.

Grigori Perelman

1966-present
Proved the Poincaré conjecture. Made fundamental contributions to topology and geometry.

Terence Tao

1975-present
Fields Medal winner. Made fundamental contributions to harmonic analysis, partial differential equations, and number theory.

Maryam Mirzakhani

1977-2017
First woman to win the Fields Medal. Made fundamental contributions to geometry and dynamical systems.

Cédric Villani

1973-present
Fields Medal winner. Made fundamental contributions to partial differential equations and optimal transport theory.

Manjul Bhargava

1974-present
Fields Medal winner. Made fundamental contributions to number theory and algebraic geometry.

Aryabhata

476-550 CE
Ancient Indian mathematician and astronomer. Made significant contributions to algebra, trigonometry, and astronomy.

Srinivasa Ramanujan

1887-1920
Self-taught mathematical genius. Made extraordinary contributions to number theory, infinite series, and continued fractions.

Nikolai Lobachevsky

1792-1856
Developed non-Euclidean geometry. Made fundamental contributions to hyperbolic geometry.

Georg Cantor

1845-1918
Created set theory. Made fundamental contributions to the theory of infinite numbers.

Richard Feynman

1918-1988
Nobel laureate in physics. Made significant contributions to quantum mechanics and mathematical physics.

Paul Dirac

1902-1984
Nobel laureate in physics. Made fundamental contributions to quantum mechanics and mathematical physics.

Niels Bohr

1885-1962
Nobel laureate in physics. Made fundamental contributions to quantum theory and atomic structure.
