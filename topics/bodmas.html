BODMAS

BODMAS is a helpful rule to remember the order of operations in math. It tells us the correct sequence to solve different operations in an expression.

BODMAS Full Form

  • B – Brackets ((), {}, [])
  • O – Orders (Powers and Square Roots)
  • D – Division
  • M – Multiplication
  • A – Addition
  • S – Subtraction

Use this rule to avoid mistakes when solving complex expressions.

Example Expression

Expression: 6 + (2 × 3)² ÷ 3 - 4

Step-by-Step Solution

  1. First, solve inside brackets: 2 × 3 = 6
  2. Now square the result: 6² = 36
  3. Then divide: 36 ÷ 3 = 12
  4. Now add: 6 + 12 = 18
  5. Finally subtract: 18 - 4 = 14

Final Answer: 14

Practice Problem

Solve using BODMAS:

8 + 12 ÷ (2 × 2) - 3

Step 1: Inside brackets: 2 × 2 = 4

Step 2: Division: 12 ÷ 4 = 3

Step 3: Addition: 8 + 3 = 11

Step 4: Subtraction: 11 - 3 = 8

Answer: 8
