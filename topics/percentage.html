Percentage

Percentage Basics

A percentage represents a part out of 100. The symbol % means "per hundred". For example, 45% means 45 out of 100.

Percentage formula:

Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100

Example: If 25 students passed out of 40, what percentage passed?

  1. Part = 25, Whole = 40
  2. Percentage = (25 / 40) × 100 = 0.625 × 100 = 62.5%

Percentage Increase and Decrease

Percentage increase or decrease shows how much a quantity has grown or shrunk in relation to its original amount.

Percentage Increase:

= ((New Value - Original Value) / Original Value) × 100

Percentage Decrease:

= ((Original Value - New Value) / Original Value) × 100

Example (Increase): A jacket price increased from $40 to $50. Find the percentage increase.

  1. Original Value = 40, New Value = 50
  2. Increase = 50 - 40 = 10
  3. Percentage Increase = (10 / 40) × 100 = 25%

Example (Decrease): A phone price dropped from $500 to $450. Find the percentage decrease.

  1. Original Value = 500, New Value = 450
  2. Decrease = 500 - 450 = 50
  3. Percentage Decrease = (50 / 500) × 100 = 10%

Converting Percentages to Fractions and Decimals

To convert a percentage to a fraction, write the percentage as a fraction with denominator 100 and simplify if possible.

To convert a percentage to a decimal, divide the percentage by 100.

Example: Convert 75% to fraction and decimal.

  1. Fraction: 75% = 75/100 = 3/4
  2. Decimal: 75% = 75 ÷ 100 = 0.75

Finding the Percentage of a Number

To find a percentage of a number, multiply the number by the percentage (as a decimal).

Formula: Percentage\ of\ Number = Number × (Percentage ÷ 100)

Example: Find 20% of 150.

  1. Convert 20% to decimal: 20 ÷ 100 = 0.2
  2. Multiply: 150 × 0.2 = 30

Word Problems on Percentage

Percentage word problems often involve finding parts, totals, or changes expressed as percentages.

Example: A shop offers a 15% discount on a $120 jacket. What is the sale price?

  1. Find discount amount: 120 × (15 ÷ 100) = 18
  2. Sale price = Original price - Discount = 120 - 18 = 102

Discounts, Profit & Loss

Discount: The reduction in price offered on the original price (marked price).

Profit: When selling price is more than cost price.

Loss: When selling price is less than cost price.

Formulas:

  • Discount = Marked Price - Selling Price
  • Profit = Selling Price - Cost Price
  • Loss = Cost Price - Selling Price
  • Profit % = (Profit / Cost Price) × 100
  • Loss % = (Loss / Cost Price) × 100
  • Discount % = (Discount / Marked Price) × 100

Example: An item costs $200 and is sold for $250. Find the profit percentage.

  1. Profit = 250 - 200 = 50
  2. Profit % = (50 / 200) × 100 = 25%

Simple Interest

Simple interest is interest calculated only on the principal amount.

Simple Interest Formula:

SI = (P × R × T) / 100

Where:

  • P = Principal amount
  • R = Rate of interest per annum (%)
  • T = Time in years

Example: Calculate simple interest on $1000 at 5% per annum for 3 years.

  1. SI = (1000 × 5 × 3) / 100 = 150

Compound Interest

Compound interest is calculated on the principal plus the interest earned so far.

Compound Interest Formula:

A = P \times \left(1 + \frac{R}{100}\right)^T

CI = A - P

Where:

  • A = Amount after interest
  • P = Principal
  • R = Rate of interest per annum (%)
  • T = Time in years

Example: Calculate compound interest on $1000 at 5% per annum for 2 years.

  1. A = 1000 × (1 + 5/100)^2 = 1000 × (1.05)^2 = 1000 × 1.1025 = 1102.5
  2. CI = 1102.5 - 1000 = 102.5

Practice Problems

1. What is 15% of 200?

Solution:

15% of 200 = (15/100) × 200 = 0.15 × 200 = 30

2. The price of a book increased from $120 to $138. Find the percentage increase.

Solution:

Increase = 138 - 120 = 18

Percentage increase = (18 / 120) × 100 = 15%

3. A laptop originally priced at $900 is sold for $765. Find the discount percentage.

Solution:

Discount = 900 - 765 = 135

Discount % = (135 / 900) × 100 = 15%

4. Calculate the simple interest on $5000 for 4 years at 6% per annum.

Solution:

SI = (5000 × 6 × 4) / 100 = 1200
