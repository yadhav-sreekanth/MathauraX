Arithmetic Fundamentals

Arithmetic is the branch of mathematics dealing with numbers and the basic operations on them: addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. It forms the foundation for all other areas of math.

Basic Arithmetic Operations

Addition (+)

Addition is the process of combining two or more numbers to get a total sum.

Example: 3 + 5 = 8

Subtraction (−)

Subtraction is the process of finding the difference between two numbers by removing the value of one from the other.

Example: 9 - 4 = 5

Multiplication (×)

Multiplication is repeated addition of the same number.

Example: 4 \times 6 = 24

Division (÷)

Division splits a number into equal parts or groups.

Example: 20 ÷ 4 = 5

Properties of Arithmetic Operations

Commutative Property

This property states that changing the order of numbers does not change the result for addition and multiplication.

Examples:

  • a + b = b + a
  • a \times b = b \times a

Associative Property

This property states that how numbers are grouped does not change the sum or product.

Examples:

  • (a + b) + c = a + (b + c)
  • (a \times b) \times c = a \times (b \times c)

Distributive Property

This property connects multiplication and addition/subtraction.

Example:

a \times (b + c) = a \times b + a \times c

Number Types

Understanding different types of numbers is essential for arithmetic.

Natural Numbers (ℕ)

Counting numbers starting from 1: 1, 2, 3, 4, ...

Whole Numbers

Natural numbers including zero: 0, 1, 2, 3, ...

Integers (ℤ)

Whole numbers including negatives: ..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...

Rational Numbers (ℚ)

Numbers that can be expressed as fractions: a/b where a and b are integers and b \neq 0.

Irrational Numbers

Numbers that cannot be expressed as a fraction of two integers, e.g., \pi, \sqrt{2}.

Order of Operations

When multiple operations appear in an expression, the order to solve them is crucial.

The commonly used acronym is PEMDAS or BODMAS:

  • P/B - Parentheses/Brackets
  • E/O - Exponents/Orders
  • MD - Multiplication and Division (left to right)
  • AS - Addition and Subtraction (left to right)

Example: Evaluate 3 + 6 \times (5 + 4) ÷ 3 - 7

  1. Solve inside parentheses: (5 + 4) = 9
  2. Multiply: 6 \times 9 = 54
  3. Divide: 54 ÷ 3 = 18
  4. Add and subtract left to right: 3 + 18 - 7 = 14

Fractions

A fraction represents a part of a whole, written as a/b, where a is numerator and b is denominator.

Types of Fractions

  • Proper Fraction: numerator < denominator (e.g., 3/4)
  • Improper Fraction: numerator ≥ denominator (e.g., 7/4)
  • Mixed Number: whole number + proper fraction (e.g., 1 \frac{3}{4})

Operations with Fractions

Addition/Subtraction: Make denominators same, then add/subtract numerators.

Multiplication: Multiply numerators, multiply denominators.

Division: Multiply by reciprocal.

Example: Add 1/3 + 2/5

  1. Find common denominator: 15
  2. Convert fractions: 1/3 = 5/15, \quad 2/5 = 6/15
  3. Add numerators: 5 + 6 = 11
  4. Result: 11/15

Decimals

Decimals are an alternative way of representing fractions using powers of ten.

Reading Decimals

Digits to the right of the decimal point represent tenths, hundredths, thousandths, etc.

Example: 3.45 = 3 + \frac{4}{10} + \frac{5}{100}

Operations with Decimals

  • Addition/Subtraction: align decimal points before operating.
  • Multiplication: multiply as whole numbers then place decimal.
  • Division: move decimal points to make divisor whole, then divide.

Percentages

Percentage means "per hundred" and is a way to express fractions with denominator 100.

Example: 45\% = \frac{45}{100} = 0.45

Calculating Percentage

To find p\% of a number N, use:

\frac{p}{100} \times N

Example: Find 20% of 150.

  1. Convert percentage to decimal: 20\% = 0.20
  2. Multiply: 0.20 \times 150 = 30
  3. Result: 30

Practice Problems

1. Calculate: 7 + 15 - 9 \times 2

Follow order of operations:

  1. Multiply first: 9 \times 2 = 18
  2. Add and subtract left to right: 7 + 15 - 18 = 4

Answer: 4

2. Simplify: \frac{3}{4} + \frac{2}{5}

  1. Common denominator: 20
  2. Convert fractions: \frac{3}{4} = \frac{15}{20}, \quad \frac{2}{5} = \frac{8}{20}
  3. Add numerators: 15 + 8 = 23
  4. Result: \frac{23}{20} = 1 \frac{3}{20}

Answer: 1 \frac{3}{20}

3. Find 15% of 240.

  1. Convert percentage: 15\% = 0.15
  2. Multiply: 0.15 \times 240 = 36

Answer: 36
