Fractions

A fraction represents a part of a whole and is expressed as a/b, where a is the numerator and b (≠ 0) is the denominator.

Types of Fractions

  • Proper Fraction: Numerator < Denominator (e.g., 3/4)
  • Improper Fraction: Numerator ≥ Denominator (e.g., 7/4)
  • Mixed Fraction: A whole number and a proper fraction (e.g., 1 3/4)

Operations on Fractions

Addition: Add numerators if denominators are same. If different, convert to common denominator first.

Subtraction: Same rule as addition.

Multiplication: Multiply numerators and denominators directly.

Division: Multiply by reciprocal of second fraction.

Step-by-Step Example

Convert a Mixed Fraction to an Improper Fraction:

  1. Multiply the whole number with the denominator: 1 × 4 = 4
  2. Add the numerator: 4 + 3 = 7
  3. Write as: 7/4

Practice Problems

1. Add: 3/5 + 2/5

Same denominator: Add numerators: 3+2 = 5

Answer: 5/5 = 1

2. Convert: 2 2/3 to an improper fraction

2 × 3 + 2 = 8

Answer: 8/3

3. Subtract: 5/6 - 1/6

Same denominator: 5 - 1 = 4

Answer: 4/6 = 2/3

4. Multiply: 2/3 × 3/4

2×3 = 6, 3×4 = 12

Answer: 6/12 = 1/2

5. Divide: 3/5 ÷ 2/3

Multiply by reciprocal: 3/5 × 3/2 = 9/10

Answer: 9/10
